Pancakes

A pancake is a flat, round cake made from a starch-based batter, typically containing flour, eggs, and milk. It is cooked on a hot surface like a griddle or frying pan and is often served for breakfast with toppings such as maple syrup and butter, or with savory fillings. Pancakes can vary in thickness depending on the recipe and are known globally by many different names

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon white sugar

Instructions

  1. Preheat a griddle or frying pan over medium heat.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt.
  3. In a separate bowl, beat the egg with the melted butter, milk, vanilla extract, and sugar.
  4. Gradually whisk the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients until just combined.
  5. Heat a small amount of butter in the griddle or frying pan.
  6. Using a ladle, pour a small amount of batter onto the hot griddle or frying pan.
  7. Cook for 2-3 minutes, or until the edges are set and the top is slightly golden brown.
  8. Flip the pancake and cook for another 1-2 minutes, or until the other side is golden brown.
  9. Repeat with the remaining batter.

Tip

For thicker pancakes, use more butter and milk. For thinner pancakes, use less butter and milk.
